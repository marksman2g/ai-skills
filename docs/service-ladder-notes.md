# AI Service Ladder Notes

Last reviewed: 2026-07-16

This file tracks the reasoning behind the AI skills website positioning.

## Strategy

The site should not pretend the owner already knows advanced AI. It should position AI skills as a practical learning-and-service ladder:

1. Learn a simple skill.
2. Build a small sample.
3. Package it as a clear offer.
4. Test whether people value it.
5. Move to the next level only after the previous level is proven.

## Current Service Ladder

### 1. Prompt Recipe Pack

- Learning curve: low
- Effort: low
- Why it sells: people want better ChatGPT/AI output but do not want to learn prompt structure.
- Best first buyers: creators, students, solo owners, admins, local businesses.
- Proof to build: 10 reusable prompts with before/after examples.

### 2. Content Cleanup

- Learning curve: low
- Effort: low-medium
- Why it sells: content creation is constant and easy for clients to understand.
- Best first buyers: small businesses, churches, nonprofits, events, service providers.
- Proof to build: captions, email drafts, bio rewrites, service descriptions.

### 3. Research Briefs

- Learning curve: low-medium
- Effort: medium
- Why it sells: people pay for decisions to be clearer and faster.
- Best first buyers: busy owners, job seekers, buyers comparing options, families making practical choices.
- Proof to build: comparison brief with sources, pros/cons, recommendation questions.

### 4. Workflow Starter Kit

- Learning curve: medium
- Effort: medium
- Why it sells: repeated admin work creates obvious time pain.
- Best first buyers: consultants, small teams, service businesses, operators.
- Proof to build: prompt + checklist + template for one repeated task.

### 5. AI Knowledge Base Setup

- Learning curve: medium-hard
- Effort: high
- Why it sells: businesses with documents, FAQs, policies, or onboarding material need consistent answers.
- Best first buyers: teams with customer support, onboarding, internal documentation, repeated questions.
- Proof to build: sample FAQ assistant or organized document-answering demo.

### 6. AI Agent Workflow Audit

- Learning curve: hard
- Effort: high
- Why it sells: reliable automation can save meaningful labor time, but only after testing and safeguards.
- Best first buyers: growing teams with repeatable operations and clear review rules.
- Proof to build: workflow map, risk checklist, tool list, and human-review design.

## Demand Signals

- Small businesses are increasingly using AI for time savings, content creation, customer communication, and data analysis.
- Recent reporting points to strong demand for agentic AI expertise, but agent work should be treated as advanced because reliability, verification, integrations, and safety matter.
- Research on workplace AI shows augmentation is more common than full automation, which supports positioning these services as human-reviewed support rather than replacement.
- AI adoption has a productivity curve: setup, training, and verification can slow results before the value appears.

## Site Positioning Rule

Do not overpromise income. Use practical labels:

- Learning curve
- Effort
- Buyer fit
- Why people pay
- Proof to build

This is stronger and safer than promising exact pay ranges.
