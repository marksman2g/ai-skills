# A1 AI Skills Newsletter

Hello,

Today's A1 AI lesson is:

**Write the instructions before you automate the task.**

The newest small-business AI signal is clear: AI is becoming more useful when it is tied to real workflows, training, prompts, partner tools, and repeatable business use cases. OpenAI announced a ChatGPT small business program on July 21, 2026, focused on hands-on training, workflows across accounting, marketing, ecommerce, and small-business operations.

That matters because most small businesses do not need a complicated AI system first.

They need their work written down clearly enough for AI to help.

## The Simple Lesson

AI cannot reliably improve a process that nobody has explained.

Before asking AI to automate anything, write a simple operating instruction:

```text
When this happens, do this.
Use this information.
Follow these rules.
Check these details.
Ask a human before this step.
```

This is the bridge between beginner AI skills and higher-value AI work.

## Use This Prompt

```text
Act like an AI operations assistant for a small business.

I want to turn this task into clear instructions AI can help with:
[describe task]

Here is what usually happens:
[describe the current process]

Create a simple AI-ready instruction sheet with:
1. Task name
2. Trigger: when this task starts
3. Inputs needed
4. Step-by-step instructions
5. Business rules
6. What AI can draft, summarize, organize, or compare
7. What a human must approve
8. Common mistakes to check
9. A reusable prompt template
```

## Example

Task: new customer inquiry.

Trigger:

- A new customer sends a message asking about services.

Inputs needed:

- Customer question
- Service list
- Prices or starting rates
- Business hours
- Booking link
- Any policy limits

AI can help:

- Summarize what the customer needs
- Draft a polite reply
- Suggest the best next step
- Turn the answer into a reusable template

Human must approve:

- Price
- Availability
- Refunds
- Legal or policy language
- Any promise made to the customer

Reusable prompt:

```text
Write a clear customer reply using only the business information below.
Do not invent prices, dates, policies, or availability.
If information is missing, write a question for the business owner instead.

Customer message:
[paste message]

Business information:
[paste approved business details]
```

## Why This Is Valuable

This skill is useful because businesses have a lot of knowledge stuck in people's heads:

- How to answer common questions
- How to qualify a lead
- How to follow up
- How to describe a service
- How to handle exceptions
- How to decide what needs approval

When you organize that knowledge, AI becomes more reliable.

That is why higher-value AI work is moving toward workflow design, business instructions, governance, and agent orchestration.

## Learning Curve

Easy:

- Write better prompts
- Create reply templates
- Summarize notes
- Clean up content

Medium:

- Turn repeated tasks into instruction sheets
- Build customer FAQ workflows
- Create research and comparison templates
- Design human review checklists

Hard:

- Build AI knowledge bases
- Connect AI to business tools
- Design agent workflows with permissions, logging, and cost controls

The hard topics can earn more because they reduce repeated labor at scale, but they require more testing and better guardrails.

## Try This Today

Pick one repeated task and answer these questions:

```text
What starts the task?

What information is needed?

What are the steps?

What rules must be followed?

What can AI draft or organize?

What must a person approve?

What mistakes would hurt trust?
```

Once you answer those, you have the beginning of an AI-ready business skill.

## A1 Takeaway

The next practical AI skill is not just asking better questions. It is turning business know-how into clear instructions that AI can follow and people can verify.

If you can document the process, you can improve it. If you can improve it, you can sell it.
